Lab testing & COAs

Scientific testing for every batch.

Every lot of Himalayan salt produced for export is tested against internationally recognised criteria. We work with ISO 17025–accredited laboratories to verify composition and safety before goods are cleared for packing.

01

Purity & mineral profile

Sodium chloride (%) and key minerals (calcium, potassium, magnesium, iron) are analysed to confirm that each batch sits within the agreed specification.

02

Heavy metals screening

Lead, arsenic, cadmium, and mercury are measured against limits guided by FAO/WHO Codex Alimentarius and destination market regulations.

03

Microbiological safety

For edible and spa-grade salt, we check total plate count, yeasts and moulds, coliforms, and specified pathogens to ensure hygienic handling and storage.

For each shipment, you receive a Certificate of Analysis (COA) linked to its lot number. Digital copies can be accessed via PDF or QR code for your internal QA records.

Traceability from mine face to sealed container.

We maintain a documented chain of custody for every order. The steps below show exactly where we control quality — and what is recorded at each stage.

Mine

Raw Himalayan rock salt is extracted from the Salt Range in Pakistan, a Triassic-era halite deposit known for its stable mineral profile.

→

Sorting

Skilled workers manually remove foreign material and visually grade crystals before they enter the processing line.

→

Washing & Crushing

Salt is washed, dried, and milled in food-grade stainless steel equipment. Grain sizes are created using calibrated sieves.

→

Grading

Fine, coarse, and extra-coarse fractions are separated and sampled. Metal detection and moisture checks are performed before packing.

→

Packing

Product is filled into sealed, batch-coded bags or retail packs. Each unit carries information needed for backward traceability.

→

Palletising & Container

Pallets are wrapped, moisture-protected, and photographed before loading. Container numbers, seal numbers, and weights are recorded for your documents.

Quality & safety FAQs.

A refined overview of the most common questions we receive from importers, QA managers, and regulatory teams about testing, certifications, and documentation.

Built for import teams

Clear answers, no marketing fluff — just how we work in practice.

Do you provide COAs for every shipment? +

Yes. Each batch is tested and issued with a Certificate of Analysis (COA). The COA number is linked to the lot code printed on bags or pallets, so you can trace every consignment back to its production records and lab results.

Which laboratories do you work with? +

We primarily work with ISO 17025–accredited laboratories such as SGS, Intertek, and national reference labs (for example PCSIR). For specific markets, we can align testing with your preferred lab or retailer requirements where needed.

Can you match our retailer or distributor specification? +

In most cases, yes. Many clients share retailer or distributor specifications for grain size, colour, or maximum levels for certain elements. We compare these with our internal limits and adapt our QC checks and reporting so your documentation aligns with what buyers expect.

How do you prove the salt is genuinely Himalayan? +

We provide origin documentation, mine references, and a consistent mineral fingerprint associated with the Salt Range deposit. For key accounts, site visits, third-party audits, or additional origin verification can be arranged.

What happens if a batch falls outside specification? +

Non-conforming batches are quarantined and never packed under export-grade labels. Corrective actions (re-sieving, re-grading, or rejection) are documented under our quality system before the order is released.
